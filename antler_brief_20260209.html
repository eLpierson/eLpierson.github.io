The Elevator Pitch

Homebuyers get inspection reports but don't know how to act on them. What's urgent? What does it cost? Who to call? Right now, real estate agents fill that gap manually - but it's not their job, and it doesn't scale.

We're building an AI-driven platform that solves both sides: helps buyers prioritize and connect with contractors, and gives agents a tool to automate what they're already doing while enhancing their trusted relationship with the client.

The unlock: For the first time, we're capturing the contractor recommendations agents have been making for years - the trusted word-of-mouth rolodex that no one in the industry has ever digitized. That's the moat.

Why This Is Big

$45B Annual work from inspection reports (US)
$2T+ US construction industry
#1 Competitor to every contractor marketplace: trusted word-of-mouth recommendations

The biggest competitor to HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack, and Angi isn't another platform - it's the agent's personal rolodex and other word-of-mouth referrals. Nobody has digitized that. We are.

The Full Lifecycle Play

The platform is branded for the agent, creating a relationship lifecycle that doesn't exist today:

1 Post-Inspection Buyer gets report. Platform triages, estimates costs, matches with trusted contractors from agent's network.
2 Homeownership Things break over time. Buyer returns to the platform - still connected to their agent.
3 Pre-Sale Ready to sell? Tons of prep work. Another contractor matching moment. Agent is top of mind.
4 Sale Homeowner uses the same agent for the sale - instead of forgetting or going with someone else.

This solves a real agent problem: client retention over the 7-10 year homeownership cycle - something no one has cracked.

Bigger Picture: Things We're Thinking About

Data Products and the "Equifax for Homes" Opportunity

Every inspection report we process is rich with structured data that nobody is aggregating today. From photos alone, we can identify appliance types, ages, and conditions - furnaces, water heaters, HVAC units, roofing materials, electrical panels, and more. Across thousands of reports, this becomes a detailed home condition rating based on count and severity of issues.

Think of it as an Equifax for homes - an objective, data-driven condition score. Who would want this? Insurance companies pricing risk, lenders underwriting mortgages, investors evaluating properties, municipalities tracking housing stock, and home warranty providers targeting the right customers. This is a long-term play, but the data starts accumulating from day one.

Objective Contractor Ratings (Before and After)

Right now, contractor ratings across every platform are subjective - based on how a homeowner is feeling, their mood, and vibes. A contractor could do excellent work and still get a mediocre review because the homeowner was stressed about something else entirely.

We have something no one else does: the before picture from the inspection report. If we capture an after picture of how the job went, we can create a more objective quality rating based on actual work performed against documented conditions. This gives contractors a genuinely differentiated value prop - a rating system that reflects their craftsmanship, not just customer sentiment.

The Homeowner's Own Rolodex

The agent's trusted contractor network seeds the platform at the start of the journey. But over time, during the homeownership years, the homeowner will inevitably collect their own trusted contractor recommendations - from neighbors, friends, coworkers, local community groups.

The platform becomes their hub. They can add their own trusted contractors to their profile and use them over time. This does two things: it deepens individual engagement with the platform, and it feeds back into the broader trusted rolodex in a more robust, community-driven way. The network of trust grows from both the top (agent) and the bottom (homeowner) simultaneously.
